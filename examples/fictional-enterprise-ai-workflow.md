# Fictional Enterprise AI Workflow Example

## Important Note

This is a fictional example created solely to demonstrate how the frameworks, workflows and templates in this repository can be applied.

The company, customer information, performance data, workflows and decisions are fictional.

No confidential employer, customer, personal or proprietary information is included.

---

## 1. Company Context

**Company:** Altura Process Systems

**Business model:** Enterprise B2B SaaS

**Solution:** Process optimization and operational intelligence platform

**Primary region:** EMEA

**Target customers:** Large industrial and technology organizations

**Primary marketing teams involved:**

- Field Marketing,
- Demand Generation,
- Product Marketing,
- Content Marketing,
- Marketing Operations,
- Sales.

Altura Process Systems operates in a complex enterprise environment with long Sales cycles, multiple buying roles and regionally differentiated customer needs.

The company wants to improve the quality and speed of campaign planning without reducing customer relevance, factual accuracy or human accountability.

---

## 2. Business Problem

Campaign teams currently collect information from multiple sources, including:

- customer interview summaries,
- Sales feedback,
- campaign performance reports,
- product messaging,
- market research,
- event feedback,
- existing content.

The process is largely manual.

This creates several challenges:

- customer insight is distributed across teams,
- information is reviewed inconsistently,
- campaign briefs take too long to prepare,
- customer problems are sometimes described too generically,
- evidence is not always traceable,
- regional teams repeat similar research,
- messaging decisions depend heavily on individual knowledge,
- content teams receive incomplete briefs.

### Business Impact

The current process contributes to:

- delayed campaign launches,
- repeated manual work,
- inconsistent message quality,
- avoidable review cycles,
- limited reuse of customer insight,
- weak documentation of assumptions,
- reduced transparency across teams.

### Problem Statement

> The current process for developing enterprise campaign briefs is manual, fragmented and inconsistent, resulting in delayed execution, repeated work and variable customer relevance. The objective is to improve speed, structure and consistency without compromising factual accuracy, customer context or human accountability.

---

## 3. Proposed AI Use Case

### Use Case Name

AI-Supported Customer Insight and Campaign Brief Workflow

### Business Process

Development of campaign briefs for enterprise demand generation and field marketing programs.

### AI-Supported Activities

AI supports:

- organizing approved source material,
- identifying recurring customer themes,
- comparing buying-role priorities,
- highlighting contradictions,
- identifying evidence gaps,
- drafting the first structure of a campaign brief,
- checking message consistency,
- identifying unsupported claims.

### AI Does Not

AI does not:

- make final campaign decisions,
- approve messaging,
- invent customer evidence,
- create product claims,
- publish content,
- decide which accounts should be targeted,
- replace Product Marketing, Sales or Legal review,
- process unapproved confidential information.

### Expected Output

A structured first-draft campaign brief containing:

- business objective,
- target audience,
- customer problems,
- desired outcomes,
- buying committee perspectives,
- value proposition,
- evidence sources,
- messaging implications,
- open questions,
- review requirements.

| Assessment Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|---|---:|---|
| Business Value | 5 | Campaign preparation is repeated frequently and affects execution speed |
| Input Readiness | 4 | Most required inputs exist but need consistent labeling |
| Technical Feasibility | 4 | Pilot can use an approved AI platform without complex integration |
| Human Review Readiness | 4 | Marketing and Product Marketing reviewers are available |
| Organizational Readiness | 3 | Teams require training and a shared workflow |
| Measurement Readiness | 4 | Current preparation time and review cycles can be measured |
| Risk | 2 | Risk is manageable with approved inputs and human review |

### Priority Decision

**Decision:** Prioritize for a controlled pilot

### Rationale

The main readiness gap is not technology.

The main gap is consistent process ownership, input preparation and reviewer adoption.

## 5. Pilot Objective

The pilot will test whether AI can improve the preparation of enterprise campaign briefs.

### Pilot Goals

- reduce time to first structured brief,
- improve consistency across briefs,
- make evidence sources more traceable,
- identify missing customer insight earlier,
- reduce avoidable content revision,
- strengthen collaboration across Marketing and Sales.

### Pilot Scope

The pilot includes:

- two fictional EMEA campaigns,
- approved internal and public source material,
- one Field Marketing user,
- one Product Marketing reviewer,
- one Marketing Operations owner,
- one final Marketing approver.

### Out of Scope

The pilot excludes:

- automated publishing,
- customer-specific personalization,
- personal customer data,
- confidential commercial information,
- automated targeting,
- lead scoring,
- external content approval,
- integration with CRM or marketing automation.

## 7. Input Preparation

Before the workflow begins, the Marketing Operations owner prepares the source material.

### Preparation Steps

1. Confirm that each source is approved.
2. Remove unnecessary personal information.
3. Label each source.
4. Add source dates.
5. Identify the target segment.
6. Separate customer evidence from internal interpretation.
7. Remove outdated messaging.
8. document missing context.
9. retain access to the original sources.

### Example Source Labels

- Source A — Customer interview summary
- Source B — Sales feedback summary
- Source C — Approved product messaging
- Source D — Campaign performance summary
- Source E — Public market research

## 8. Workflow Design

| Workflow Stage | AI Role | Human Role | Accountable Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Define campaign objective | None | Set business objective | Field Marketing Lead |
| Prepare inputs | None | Validate and label sources | Marketing Operations |
| Synthesize insight | Identify themes and gaps | Validate customer context | Product Marketing |
| Draft campaign brief | Create first structured draft | Review and correct | Field Marketing |
| Validate claims | Flag unsupported statements | Confirm product and evidence accuracy | Product Marketing |
| Review messaging | Compare against approved messaging | Refine customer relevance | Product Marketing |
| Approve final brief | None | Approve or reject | Marketing Director |
| Measure workflow | Structure results | Evaluate value and adoption | Marketing Operations |

---

## 9. AI Instruction

The pilot uses an instruction based on the following structure:

> Review the approved source material and create a first structured campaign brief.
>
> Use only the information provided.
>
> Identify recurring customer business problems, desired outcomes, buying barriers and stakeholder differences.
>
> Reference the source label supporting each major conclusion.
>
> Separate direct evidence from interpretation.
>
> Do not invent facts, customer quotations, product capabilities, statistics or proof points.
>
> Highlight contradictions, missing information and unsupported assumptions.
>
> Assign a confidence level of High, Medium or Low to major customer insights.
>
> Do not make final campaign or investment decisions.
>
> Present the output using the approved campaign brief structure.

## 15. Corrections Identified During Review

The first AI-supported draft contains several issues.

### Issue 1: Unsupported Outcome Claim

The draft states that the platform reduces operating cost by a specific percentage.

No approved evidence supports this claim.

**Decision:** Remove the claim.

### Issue 2: Overgeneralized Customer Need

The draft treats all industrial companies as having the same transformation maturity.

**Decision:** Add a maturity distinction and avoid broad generalization.

### Issue 3: Missing Procurement Perspective

The draft does not address commercial risk or procurement.

**Decision:** Add procurement as a buying role and document the evidence gap.

### Issue 4: Generic Call to Action

The initial call to action is “Learn more.”

**Decision:** Replace it with a specific executive workshop invitation.

### Issue 5: Weak Source Traceability

Two conclusions do not reference supporting sources.

**Decision:** Mark them as low confidence until sources are identified.

## 20. Pilot Decision

### Decision

**Continue and refine the pilot**

### Rationale

The workflow creates measurable process value and improves consistency.

However, scaling requires:

- clearer input standards,
- reviewer training,
- a shared terminology list,
- stronger version control,
- documented rejection criteria,
- continued human approval.

### Conditions Before Scaling

1. Approve the input checklist.
2. Train all pilot users.
3. Define required source labels.
4. Assign final approval roles.
5. Document prohibited data.
6. Establish workflow monitoring.
7. Review quality after three additional campaigns.

---

## 21. Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Business Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Unsupported claims | Brand and credibility risk | Require evidence validation |
| Sensitive information | Privacy or confidentiality risk | Use approved and anonymized inputs |
| Generic output | Low customer relevance | Use segment-specific context and human review |
| Overreliance on AI | Reduced judgment | Keep final accountability with named owners |
| Weak source quality | Incorrect conclusions | Document confidence and evidence gaps |
| Inconsistent adoption | Limited value | Train users and integrate into existing processes |
| Unclear version control | Draft used externally | Separate draft and approved versions |

---

## 22. Scaling Recommendation

The workflow may scale gradually.

### Phase 1: Controlled Pilot

- limited users,
- approved campaigns,
- manual input preparation,
- mandatory review,
- documented measurement.

### Phase 2: Standardized Workflow

- shared templates,
- reviewer training,
- consistent source labels,
- formal approval process,
- adoption reporting.

### Phase 3: Selective Integration

Potential future integration may include:

- approved content libraries,
- campaign planning tools,
- performance dashboards,
- workflow documentation.

Integration should only occur after governance and process adoption are stable.

## Key Learning

AI creates value in enterprise B2B marketing when it improves a clearly defined process.

In this fictional example, the strongest contribution is not autonomous content creation.

The strongest contribution is:

- structured synthesis,
- faster preparation,
- improved consistency,
- better source traceability,
- earlier identification of evidence gaps.

The workflow remains dependent on:

- approved inputs,
- customer context,
- subject-matter expertise,
- human review,
- final accountability,
- measurable adoption.
