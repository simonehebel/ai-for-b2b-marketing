# AI for B2B Marketing

Practical frameworks and human-in-the-loop workflows for applying artificial intelligence in complex B2B and enterprise marketing environments.

## Purpose

This repository explores how AI can support B2B marketing teams in structured, responsible and commercially relevant ways.

The focus is not on AI as a collection of isolated tools or prompts.

The focus is on designing workflows that connect:

- clear business objectives,
- reliable input data,
- human judgment,
- defined responsibilities,
- quality control,
- measurable outcomes,
- organizational adoption.

## My Point of View

AI creates value in B2B marketing when it improves the quality, speed or consistency of a defined business process.

It should not replace strategic judgment, customer understanding or accountability.

In complex enterprise environments, effective AI adoption requires more than access to technology. Teams need clear use cases, validated inputs, human review, governance and realistic operating models.

The materials in this repository are designed around that principle.

## Who This Is For

The materials are relevant for:

- B2B and enterprise marketing leaders,
- go-to-market and transformation teams,
- demand generation and lifecycle teams,
- content and messaging teams,
- marketing operations,
- field and regional marketing,
- Sales and Marketing stakeholders,
- organizations introducing AI into existing marketing processes.

## AI Use Case Principles

### 1. Start with the Business Problem

Do not begin with the tool.

Define the decision, workflow or business outcome that needs improvement.

### 2. Use Reliable Inputs

AI output quality depends on the relevance, completeness and accuracy of the information provided.

### 3. Keep Human Accountability

A named person should remain accountable for reviewing, approving and using the output.

### 4. Separate Drafting from Approval

AI may support research, synthesis or drafting.

Final approval should remain with the appropriate subject-matter or business owner.

### 5. Protect Sensitive Information

Confidential customer, employee, commercial or proprietary information should not be entered into unapproved systems.

### 6. Validate Claims

Business claims, statistics, customer statements and market information should be verified before publication or decision-making.

### 7. Measure Business Value

AI use cases should be evaluated against outcomes such as:

- time saved,
- improved quality,
- greater consistency,
- faster execution,
- better customer relevance,
- reduced rework,
- increased adoption.

## What This Repository Is Not

This repository is not:

- a generic prompt collection,
- a list of AI tools,
- a software engineering portfolio,
- a substitute for legal, security or compliance review,
- a source of confidential customer or employer information,
- a claim that AI should replace marketing judgment.

## Suggested AI Workflow

A practical AI-enabled marketing workflow may follow these stages:

1. Define the business objective.
2. Identify the users and process owners.
3. Validate the available inputs.
4. Define the role of AI.
5. Document risks and restrictions.
6. Generate or synthesize the first output.
7. Review the output against agreed criteria.
8. Correct, approve or reject the output.
9. Use the result within the wider marketing process.
10. Measure value and improve the workflow.

## Success Criteria

A useful AI-enabled marketing workflow should be:

- relevant to a real business need,
- understandable to the people using it,
- supported by reliable inputs,
- repeatable,
- reviewable,
- measurable,
- compliant with organizational requirements,
- realistic to adopt.
