# Customer Insight Synthesis Workflow

## Purpose

This workflow helps B2B marketing teams use AI to structure and synthesize qualitative customer insight from approved source material.

The workflow can support:

- campaign planning,
- messaging development,
- customer journey design,
- content strategy,
- segment analysis,
- Sales enablement,
- product marketing,
- go-to-market planning.

AI supports the organization and synthesis of information.

Human reviewers remain responsible for:

- source quality,
- interpretation,
- factual accuracy,
- customer relevance,
- business conclusions,
- final approval.

## When to Use This Workflow

Use this workflow when relevant customer information is distributed across several approved sources, such as:

- customer interview notes,
- Sales call summaries,
- event feedback,
- customer surveys,
- win-loss findings,
- customer advisory board notes,
- customer success feedback,
- market research,
- approved public sources.

The workflow can help identify:

- recurring customer problems,
- desired business outcomes,
- buying barriers,
- stakeholder differences,
- customer terminology,
- evidence gaps,
- potential messaging themes,
- areas requiring further research.

## When Not to Use This Workflow

Do not use this workflow when:

- the source material is not approved for AI processing,
- personal or confidential information cannot be protected,
- the source quality is unclear,
- the sample is too small to support broader conclusions,
- the workflow would replace direct customer research,
- legal or compliance interpretation is required,
- findings would be published without human review,
- individual customer statements would be treated as representative evidence.

## 4. Prepare the Source Material

Before using AI:

1. Remove information that is not required.
2. Remove or anonymize personal information.
3. Remove confidential information that is not approved.
4. Separate direct evidence from interpretation.
5. Add source labels.
6. Add dates where relevant.
7. Document the customer segment or context.
8. Identify missing context.
9. Retain access to the original source for validation.

### Recommended Source Labels

Use labels such as:

- Source A — Customer interview
- Source B — Sales call summary
- Source C — Survey response
- Source D — Win-loss interview
- Source E — Event feedback

### Important Principle

Do not combine source material into an anonymous block without traceability.

The reviewer should be able to identify which source supports each conclusion.

## 5. Define the AI Task

### AI May Support

- grouping similar observations,
- identifying recurring themes,
- separating facts from assumptions,
- comparing stakeholder perspectives,
- extracting customer terminology,
- identifying contradictions,
- highlighting evidence gaps,
- producing a first structured summary.

### AI Must Not

AI must not:

- invent customer evidence,
- claim statistical significance,
- treat one statement as representative,
- create quotations that do not exist,
- infer confidential information,
- make final strategic decisions,
- publish findings without review,
- hide uncertainty.

## 6. Recommended Analysis Structure

### A. Customer Business Problems

For each problem, document:

- description,
- affected customer role,
- business impact,
- evidence source,
- frequency within the available material,
- confidence level.

### B. Desired Outcomes

For each outcome, document:

- desired business result,
- relevant stakeholder,
- expected value,
- evidence source,
- confidence level.

### C. Buying Barriers

Possible barriers include:

- budget,
- internal ownership,
- technology concerns,
- integration,
- risk,
- procurement,
- change readiness,
- unclear ROI,
- competing priorities.

### E. Customer Language

Document:

- recurring terms,
- problem descriptions,
- desired outcomes,
- objections,
- terminology to avoid.

Do not create direct quotations unless the source contains the exact wording.

### F. Contradictions

Identify where sources disagree.

Examples:

- different priorities across roles,
- regional differences,
- conflicting perceptions,
- Sales interpretation versus customer statement,
- short-term versus long-term needs.

### G. Evidence Gaps

Examples:

- no evidence from economic buyers,
- insufficient regional coverage,
- outdated interviews,
- no proof of urgency,
- limited information about procurement,
- unclear business impact.

## 7. Confidence Levels

### High Confidence

Use when:

- the theme appears across several relevant sources,
- evidence is recent,
- context is consistent,
- customer evidence is direct,
- there are no major contradictions.

### Medium Confidence

Use when:

- some supporting evidence exists,
- the theme appears in a limited number of sources,
- context is partially incomplete,
- further validation would be useful.

### Low Confidence

Use when:

- evidence is weak,
- the conclusion is based on one source,
- the source is indirect,
- context is missing,
- contradictory evidence exists.

## 8. Recommended AI Instruction

> Review the approved source material and identify recurring customer business problems, desired outcomes, buying barriers, stakeholder differences and evidence gaps.
>
> Separate direct evidence from interpretation.
>
> Do not invent quotations, facts or customer statements.
>
> Reference the source label supporting each conclusion.
>
> Assign a confidence level of High, Medium or Low.
>
> Highlight contradictions and missing information.
>
> Do not make final strategic recommendations.
>
> Produce the output using the agreed analysis structure.

The instruction should be adapted to the organization, source material and approved AI platform.

### Reviewer Questions

- Does every major conclusion have supporting evidence?
- Are sources correctly represented?
- Has AI created unsupported assumptions?
- Are isolated statements treated as isolated?
- Are contradictions visible?
- Are confidence levels realistic?
- Is customer language accurate?
- Are direct quotations exact?
- Are evidence gaps documented?
- Is further research required?
- Can the output support the intended business decision?

## 11. Rejection Criteria

Reject or revise the output when:

- sources cannot be traced,
- conclusions are unsupported,
- direct quotations are invented,
- individual statements are generalized,
- customer roles are confused,
- context is missing,
- sensitive information is exposed,
- confidence is overstated,
- important contradictions are omitted,
- the output does not support the business objective.

## Key Principle

AI can accelerate the synthesis of customer insight.

It should not replace:

- direct customer research,
- source validation,
- contextual understanding,
- human judgment,
- accountability.

The value of the workflow depends on:

- approved inputs,
- traceable evidence,
- realistic confidence,
- structured human review,
- clear business application.
