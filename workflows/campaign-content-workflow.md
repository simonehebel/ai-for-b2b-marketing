# AI-Supported Campaign Content Workflow

## Purpose

This workflow helps B2B marketing teams use AI to support the structured development of campaign content.

The workflow connects:

- business objectives,
- customer insight,
- audience priorities,
- approved messaging,
- content requirements,
- human review,
- quality assurance,
- performance measurement.

AI may support research, structuring, drafting and quality checks.

Human owners remain responsible for:

- campaign strategy,
- customer relevance,
- factual accuracy,
- product claims,
- brand consistency,
- legal and compliance requirements,
- final approval,
- publication.

## When to Use This Workflow

Use this workflow when developing content for:

- demand generation campaigns,
- account-based marketing programs,
- lifecycle nurture,
- regional campaigns,
- product or solution launches,
- executive engagement,
- events and webinars,
- Sales enablement,
- customer expansion programs.

The workflow is especially useful when teams need to:

- create consistent campaign assets,
- adapt global messaging for regional audiences,
- improve briefing quality,
- accelerate first drafts,
- reuse validated customer insight,
- strengthen review and approval,
- reduce avoidable rework.

## When Not to Use This Workflow

Do not use this workflow when:

- the business objective is unclear,
- the target audience is not defined,
- approved product information is unavailable,
- customer data is not approved for AI processing,
- external content would be published without human review,
- legal or compliance approval is required but unavailable,
- AI is expected to create customer evidence,
- unsupported claims would be used,
- the workflow replaces customer or subject-matter expertise.

## 8. Define the Role of AI

### AI May Support

AI may support:

- structuring the first draft,
- adapting approved messaging by audience,
- summarizing approved source material,
- producing alternative headlines,
- improving readability,
- identifying missing sections,
- checking terminology consistency,
- suggesting calls to action,
- creating first-draft variants,
- supporting localization preparation.

### AI Must Not

AI must not:

- invent product features,
- invent customer quotes,
- create unsupported claims,
- publish content,
- approve content,
- process restricted data in unapproved systems,
- replace Legal or Compliance review,
- make final targeting decisions,
- create false urgency,
- present assumptions as facts.

## 9. Recommended AI Instruction

A structured instruction may follow this format:

> Create a first draft of the requested campaign asset using only the approved inputs provided.
>
> Follow the defined target audience, customer problem, value proposition, message hierarchy, tone and call to action.
>
> Do not invent facts, customer statements, product capabilities, statistics or proof points.
>
> Clearly identify any missing information or unsupported claim.
>
> Use customer-focused language and avoid generic marketing phrases.
>
> Produce the output in the requested format.
>
> The draft will be reviewed and approved by a human owner before use.

The instruction should be adapted to the asset, organization and approved AI platform.

## 10. Drafting Workflow

### Step 1: Prepare the Brief

Confirm:

- business objective,
- target audience,
- customer problem,
- value proposition,
- message hierarchy,
- call to action,
- approved evidence,
- asset requirements.

### Step 2: Prepare Approved Inputs

- remove unnecessary information,
- remove unapproved personal or confidential data,
- label sources,
- validate product information,
- confirm proof points,
- confirm brand requirements.

### Step 3: Generate the First Draft

Use AI to produce:

- a structured first version,
- alternative headlines where relevant,
- audience-specific variants,
- identified information gaps.

### Step 4: Review the Draft

The reviewer checks:

- accuracy,
- customer relevance,
- message consistency,
- product claims,
- tone,
- completeness,
- required approvals.

### Step 5: Revise

AI may support revision based on documented human feedback.

The reviewer remains responsible for confirming that corrections were implemented correctly.

### Step 6: Approve

The final approver accepts or rejects the content.

### Step 7: Publish or Activate

Only the approved version may be used.

### Step 8: Measure

Review performance and document learnings.

## 12. Content Quality Checklist

Before approval, confirm:

### Business Relevance

- [ ] The asset supports the campaign objective
- [ ] The target audience is clear
- [ ] The call to action is relevant
- [ ] The content supports the intended customer journey stage

### Customer Relevance

- [ ] The customer problem is accurately represented
- [ ] The language reflects customer priorities
- [ ] The message is differentiated by buying role where required
- [ ] Generic or irrelevant content was removed

### Accuracy

- [ ] Product information is correct
- [ ] Statistics were verified
- [ ] Customer evidence is approved
- [ ] No facts or quotations were invented
- [ ] Assumptions are clearly identified

### Messaging and Brand

- [ ] The value proposition is clear
- [ ] Claims are supported
- [ ] Tone and terminology are consistent
- [ ] The content is readable
- [ ] The asset follows brand guidelines

### Governance

- [ ] Only approved inputs were used
- [ ] Sensitive information was handled correctly
- [ ] Required reviewers completed the review
- [ ] Required disclaimers are included
- [ ] Final approval is documented

## 13. Rejection Criteria

Reject or revise the content when:

- the customer problem is unclear,
- content is generic,
- product information is incorrect,
- claims are unsupported,
- customer evidence is invented,
- the tone is inappropriate,
- sensitive information is exposed,
- the call to action is unclear,
- required context is missing,
- required reviews are incomplete,
- the asset does not support the campaign objective.

## 14. Localization Workflow

AI may support localization preparation, but local review remains required.

### Localization Inputs

- approved source content,
- regional messaging guidance,
- terminology list,
- cultural considerations,
- legal requirements,
- local proof points,
- channel requirements.

### Localization Process

1. Confirm the approved source version.
2. Identify content that requires adaptation rather than translation.
3. Provide regional context and terminology.
4. Generate a first localized draft.
5. Review language and cultural relevance.
6. Validate product and legal claims.
7. Approve the local version.
8. Monitor performance by market.

### Localization Checklist

- [ ] The source version is approved
- [ ] The language is accurate
- [ ] Local terminology is correct
- [ ] Customer context is relevant
- [ ] Cultural assumptions were reviewed
- [ ] Local proof points are approved
- [ ] Legal requirements are met
- [ ] Final local approval is documented

## Key Principle

AI can accelerate campaign content development.

It should not replace:

- campaign strategy,
- customer understanding,
- subject-matter expertise,
- factual validation,
- brand judgment,
- human accountability.

The value of the workflow depends on:

- a strong campaign brief,
- approved inputs,
- clear message hierarchy,
- structured review,
- documented approval,
- measurable outcomes.
